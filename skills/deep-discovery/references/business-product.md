# Business / Product Pattern

Use this pattern when evaluating a business strategy, product idea, market
entry plan, go-to-market motion, pricing model, or startup concept.

## Foundation (Q1-Q10)

- What is the goal, and how do we get there?
- Who is the customer and what pain are we solving?
- Why now? What changed?
- What is the business model?
- What is the market size?
- Who are the existing players?
- What is the unfair advantage?
- What does the MVP look like?
- What is the timeline to revenue?
- What resources are available?

## Mechanics (Q11-Q30)

- Customer acquisition strategy
- Pricing model
- Cost structure
- Key partnerships needed
- Technical requirements
- Regulatory considerations
- Go-to-market plan
- Success metrics and KPIs
- Team structure needed
- Funding requirements

## Stress Testing (Q31-Q50)

- What if a large competitor enters?
- What if customer acquisition costs double?
- What if the market shrinks?
- What if key team members leave?
- What if funding falls through?
- What if the regulatory environment changes?
- What is the worst-case scenario and can we survive it?

## Competitive / Alternative Analysis (Q51-Q65)

- What existing products, services, or workflows already address this pain?
- Why would customers switch from the default alternative?
- What would a well-funded competitor copy first?
- What advantage is hard to copy?
- What distribution channel could become a bottleneck?
- What pricing, packaging, or positioning assumptions are unproven?
- What would make this idea irrelevant in 12 months?

## Feasibility (Q66-Q80)

- Can the founding team actually execute this?
- What is the minimum viable team?
- What needs to be true for this to work?
- What is the path to first 10 customers? First 100?
- What partnerships are required vs nice-to-have?
- What is the burn rate and runway?
- What regulatory approvals are needed?

## Refinement (Q81-Q90)

- What features can be cut from the MVP?
- What is the simplest possible version that validates the hypothesis?
- What would a customer pay for today, not someday?
- What operational complexity can be eliminated?

## Synthesis (Q91-Q100)

- Revised go-to-market plan
- Realistic timeline with milestones
- Top 3 existential risks and mitigations
- Honest assessment: is this worth pursuing?
